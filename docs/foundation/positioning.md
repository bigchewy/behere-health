# POSITIONING DOCUMENT: BeHere.health

## 1. COMPETITIVE ALTERNATIVES
If BeHere.health didn't exist, Long COVID/POTS patients seeking mindset support would use:

**PRIMARY:** ChatGPT and generic AI tools. This is the default destination for desperate patients who will try anything. Patients know the responses are generic and somewhat out of touch with their reality, but cycle back because nothing better exists. They are not loyal to ChatGPT. They are stuck with it.

**SECONDARY:** YouTube videos and Reddit threads. Unstructured, inconsistent, and algorithmically random rather than therapeutically matched to emotional state. Patients graze here out of desperation, not because it works.

**TERTIARY:** Specialized chronic illness programs (Primal Trust, The Gupta Program) for patients who find them and can afford them. Traditional therapy when accessible, which for this population is often not.

The critical insight: patients are not loyal to ChatGPT. They are stuck with it. That is a very different competitive situation.

A second critical insight: programs like Primal Trust and The Gupta Program are not the competition. They are the setup. Those programs sell frameworks and content. They teach the map. BeHere.health is the guide that walks patients through the territory in real time. Most patients who complete or follow those programs are doing the practice work alone, journaling, re-watching videos, hoping they're applying the framework correctly. BeHere.health fills that gap.

## 2. UNIQUE ATTRIBUTES
BeHere.health uniquely combines:

- **Six named therapeutic advisors, each rooted in a distinct established framework.** Current frameworks include ACT, Seligman's ABCDE, the Hoffman Process Quadrinity Check, Byron Katie's The Work, and Jim Loehr's energy management. New frameworks can be added with low effort as the library grows.
- **Emotional-state routing:** A single opening question ("How are you feeling today?") determines which advisor responds. Patients in crisis get a different framework than patients in a stable, growth-oriented moment.
- **Voice-first interaction** designed for patients with brain fog, fatigue, and mobility limitations on bad symptom days.
- **Post-viral syndrome context** built into every response, including understanding of symptom unpredictability and medical gaslighting dynamics.
- **The practice layer that content programs don't provide.** Programs like Primal Trust deliver training and frameworks. BeHere.health delivers the interactive, real-time experience of actually working through those frameworks with a guide. Patients voice their situation, receive dynamic feedback, and go emotionally deeper than solo journaling or passive content consumption allows.
- **Interactive somatic and nervous system exercises.** Beyond conversation, BeHere.health can guide patients through active practices such as vagal nerve stimulation work: voo breathing, eye gaze exercises, and other body-based interventions that require real-time guidance to execute well. This moves the product from mindset coaching into embodied practice support.

No alternative offers this combination. ChatGPT blends all frameworks into a generic average that represents none of them well. Specialized programs lack AI personalization, voice interaction, and real-time practice guidance. Traditional therapy lacks accessibility and often disease-specific understanding.

## 3. VALUE
BeHere.health delivers something most Long COVID/POTS patients have never found in their illness journey: a genuine perspective shift, and a way to actually practice the frameworks they've been trying to learn alone.

These patients are suffering twice. Once from their symptoms, and again from the exhausting, futile effort of fighting those symptoms. The product's core value is helping patients move from resistance to acceptance, from fighting their body to being in alignment with it.

The measure of a session working is not task completion. It is a shift in how the patient relates to their own experience. Patients describe finishing sessions in tears, not from sadness, but from release. Something unlocks. A perspective shifts. The fight softens.

In practice this looks like:
- **Emotional release and perspective shift** after sessions, not just information or coping tips
- **A different relationship with symptoms**, grounded in surrender and presence rather than battle
- **Feeling understood** by a tool that knows their specific medical reality, not generic mental health content
- **Consistent access to structured mindset frameworks** without depending on therapist availability, insurance, or physical capacity to leave home
- **The ability to actually do the work**, not just understand it. Patients who have read about ACT or watched Primal Trust videos now have a guide helping them apply the framework to their specific situation in real time
- **Guided somatic practice** for nervous system regulation, with real-time instruction through exercises like voo breathing and eye gaze work that are difficult to self-direct without feedback

## 4. TARGET CUSTOMER
BeHere.health serves two distinct segments, both highly motivated but at different points in their journey.

**Segment 1: Early-journey patients (weeks to months into Long COVID/POTS)**
- The medical system is failing them fast and they are overwhelmed
- Desperate for anything that helps, willing to try non-medical approaches early
- The hook: hope and a sense of being understood when the system isn't delivering either
- Characteristics: active researchers, heavy online community participation, open to mindset approaches if framed carefully

**Segment 2: Long-time sufferers (years into illness)**
- Have exhausted medical options and are grasping for resolution
- Battle-hardened and skeptical, but willing to spend time and money on anything with a credible framework
- The hook: something that actually works after everything else hasn't
- Characteristics: higher willingness to pay, deeper frustration, strong word-of-mouth potential when something breaks through

**A high-value sub-segment within both groups: existing Primal Trust and Gupta Program participants.** These patients have already bought into the framework. They understand the mind-body connection. They are actively trying to practice. They are the warmest possible audience for a tool that helps them practice better. They do not need to be convinced of the approach; they need a better way to execute it.

**Shared psychographic across all segments:**
- Tech-comfortable and self-advocacy oriented
- Have experienced medical dismissal or gaslighting
- Active in online support communities (Reddit, Facebook groups)
- Face barriers to traditional therapy: cost, energy, skepticism toward generic approaches

## 5. MARKET CATEGORY
**Mindset support for Long COVID and POTS patients**

This positions BeHere.health not as another therapy app or AI tool, but as something built specifically for this patient population. The category leads with patient identity, not product type. When a Long COVID patient encounters this category framing, the immediate reaction should be recognition: "this was built for me," not "this is another mental health app."

This is how Primal Trust carved out "nervous system healing for chronic illness" as a category. BeHere.health goes narrower and more specific: mindset coaching that understands post-viral syndrome, symptom unpredictability, and the particular exhaustion of medical gaslighting. The AI coaching and voice interaction are delivery mechanisms, not the category definition.

Importantly, BeHere.health occupies a distinct layer in the ecosystem rather than competing directly with content programs. Those programs are the curriculum. BeHere.health is the practice environment. Patients who use both get more from each.

Narrowing to Long COVID and POTS now does not prevent expansion into ME/CFS and other post-viral syndromes later. It ensures the initial positioning is sharp enough to be credible and resonant with the highest-need segment first.

## 6. WHY NOW

**The treatment gap is acute, not gradual.** Millions of Long COVID and POTS patients are living with documented anxiety and depression right now. Specialized mental health resources that understand post-viral syndrome are nearly nonexistent. These patients are not waiting to be found: they are already online, already organized, already sharing coping strategies in large, active communities. The demand is present and concentrated. The supply is not. That gap is the primary reason this product exists today rather than in five years.

**AI acceptance has already removed the adoption barrier.** The "will patients trust an AI for emotional support?" objection is largely resolved. Mainstream adoption of AI tools for mental health has moved faster than most predicted, and this population, tech-comfortable and blocked from traditional care by cost, energy, and scheduling barriers, is already using generic AI tools as a substitute. The question is no longer whether they will engage with AI. It is whether the AI they are using was built for them.

**Medical recognition is building as a tailwind.** Post-viral syndromes are gaining clinical legitimacy, with research, advocacy, and institutional attention accelerating since 2020. That recognition reduces the stigma around mind-body approaches and gives patients permission to pursue mindset-based support without feeling dismissed. This does not create the market. It removes a real barrier to entering it.

The sequencing matters: an acute supply gap creates the urgency, established AI behavior removes the adoption friction, and growing clinical validation lowers the credibility barrier. All three are present now.
