## Awareness-Level SEO Framework

**Crisis State (Actively Suffering)**
Who they are: Searching "long covid depression suicidal" or "pots making me crazy" — in acute distress, desperate for anything that acknowledges their reality
H1 framing approach: Immediate validation + specific condition acknowledgment. "Long COVID Depression Feels Different Because It Is Different"
Opening paragraph strategy: First 150 words must name the specific experience (medical gaslighting, symptom unpredictability, identity loss) before offering any solution. Establish "you are not imagining this" credibility first.
Service link placement: No direct product links. Single soft link to "support designed for POTS patients" in final paragraph only.

**Problem-Aware (Seeking Understanding)**
Who they are: Searching "why does POTS cause depression" or "long covid mental health connection" — need frameworks to understand their experience
H1 framing approach: Problem explanation with condition-specific insight. "Why POTS Depression Differs from Standard Depression"
Opening paragraph strategy: Lead with the question they're asking, provide the physiological/psychological connection they can't find elsewhere
Service link placement: Methodology or framework link mid-article where solution naturally fits

**Solution-Aware (Researching Approaches)**
Who they are: Searching "acceptance therapy for chronic illness" or "POTS therapy techniques" — know mindset work helps, need specific methods
H1 framing approach: Method differentiation. "Acceptance Commitment Therapy for POTS: Why Generic ACT Isn't Enough"
Opening paragraph strategy: Acknowledge they've tried things that haven't worked, position specialized approach
Service link placement: Multiple contextual links to specific therapeutic approaches, demo or trial offer

**Practice-Seeking (Ready to Engage)**
Who they are: Searching "ACT therapy exercises for POTS" or "acceptance meditation scripts chronic illness" — ready for implementation
H1 framing approach: Outcome-focused differentiation. "The POTS-Specific ACT Framework That Actually Works"
Opening paragraph strategy: Promise specific, actionable differentiation from generic approaches they've found
Service link placement: Multiple product links, strong CTA, free trial or assessment offer

**Validation-Seeking (Proof-Oriented)**
Who they are: Searching "POTS recovery stories therapy" or "long covid mindset healing success" — need evidence before committing
H1 framing approach: Story-driven proof. "How Sarah's POTS Anxiety Improved Through Acceptance Therapy"
Opening paragraph strategy: Story hook that demonstrates specific transformation relevant to their symptoms
Service link placement: Program links within story context, testimonial-driven CTAs

## Search Language Rules

**Vocabulary Split**
Headings/H1s: Patient emotional language. "POTS is ruining my life," "Long COVID depression feels different," "I'm so tired of fighting my body." Body copy: Clinical terminology for credibility. "Postural orthostatic tachycardia syndrome," "acceptance-based interventions," "autonomic dysfunction," "post-viral syndrome manifestation."

Objection acknowledgment vocabulary: "This isn't another 'think positive' approach," "You've probably tried meditation before and it didn't work," "Your symptoms are real and this doesn't minimize them," "This complements medical treatment, doesn't replace it."

**VOC Sourcing**
Reddit communities (r/POTS, r/covidlonghaulers, r/cfs): Extract emotional phrases ("I feel like I'm losing my mind," "nobody understands how hard this is"), failed-attempt language ("tried therapy but they don't get it," "meditation makes me more anxious"), specific symptom descriptions.

Facebook support groups: Mine unanswered questions about mental health approaches, objection language around "woo-woo" treatments, desperation indicators ("willing to try anything").

Medical forum threads: Extract gaps in clinical advice, patient complaints about psychiatric referrals, terminology patients use vs. clinical language.

Patient advocacy sites comments: Collect language around medical gaslighting, identity loss, hope/hopelessness spectrum.

**Meta Description Rules**
Swap test: Could BetterHelp use this description? If yes, add condition-specific detail.
Specificity test: Must contain POTS/Long COVID + mindset/therapy + unique differentiation.
Template format: "[Condition] patients face [specific challenge]. [Unique approach] designed for [symptom reality] helps [specific outcome]. [Credibility marker]."
Example: "POTS depression includes symptom unpredictability standard therapy doesn't address. Acceptance-based framework designed for autonomic dysfunction helps break anxiety-symptom cycles. Developed specifically for post-viral patients."

## Phase 1 Targets & Exclusion Zone

**Phase 1 Targets (0-6 months)**
Keyword clusters: Crisis support + acceptance therapy methods. These clusters have minimal competition because authority sites avoid crisis language and condition-specific therapy frameworks don't exist.

SERP features: HowTo boxes for "how to break POTS anxiety spiral" (5-7 numbered steps, 40-60 words each). PAA targeting for non-medical queries: "why does POTS cause depression," "what therapy works for chronic illness." Long-tail featured snippets for specific technique queries: "acceptance meditation script for POTS patients" (75-100 word definition format).

Format rules: HowTo steps must include physiological rationale (why this works for POTS specifically). PAA answers lead with condition validation before methodology. Featured snippet content uses clinical terminology in first sentence, patient language in elaboration.

**E-E-A-T Positioning**
Scope-of-practice transparency: Clear boundaries between mindset support and medical treatment on every page. "This complements medical care" disclaimers in headers and footers.

Author authority approach: Patient advocate + therapeutic framework expertise rather than clinical credentials. Emphasize lived experience with post-viral conditions + training in specific modalities (ACT, Hoffman Process, etc.).

Launch-day schema: LocalBusiness schema (not MedicalBusiness), ProfessionalService markup, FAQPage schema for objection-handling content.

**Exclusion Zone**
High-competition queries: "POTS treatment," "long COVID recovery," "chronic illness therapy" — authority sites (Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic) own page 1. Revisit at 6 months with domain authority.

Brand-adjacent terms: "Primal Trust alternative," "Gupta Program vs," "DNRS comparison" — positioning as complement, not competitor, until established.

Competitive comparison content: "Best therapy apps for chronic illness," "Top mindset programs POTS" — requires credibility to make comparative claims.

Medical advice positioning: Any content that could be interpreted as diagnosis, treatment recommendation, or medical guidance. Focus on mindset and emotional support only.

Featured snippets for high-authority queries: "POTS symptoms," "long COVID diagnosis," medical definition queries — medical sites own these permanently.
