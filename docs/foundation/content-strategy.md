# Content Strategy Document: BeHere.health

## Content Tilt

**"The practice layer that content programs don't provide."**

BeHere.health occupies the gap between understanding mindset frameworks and actually working through them. Primal Trust, the Gupta Program, and similar content platforms deliver training and frameworks. Patients watch the videos, read the materials, understand the concepts, then sit alone trying to execute solo journaling or meditation practices. The shift from information consumption to real-time practice is where most patients get stuck.

Our content tilt addresses this execution gap specifically. We don't compete on framework explanations — ACT principles, polyvagal theory basics, or general chronic illness mindset concepts. We own the "what to do with those frameworks when it's 2pm, you've overdone it, you're in the middle of a crash, and there's no one to guide you through it" space. Every piece of content assumes the reader has encountered mindset approaches before and focuses on the practical execution layer.

This positions us as complementary to, not competitive with, established programs. Patients who complete those programs become our warmest audience because they understand the frameworks but need guided practice. The tilt creates content noncompetition — we reference and build on existing programs rather than explaining foundational concepts from scratch.

The emotional cost of the execution gap matters here. Understanding a framework but being unable to apply it during a crash doesn't just leave patients stuck. It adds a layer of failure on top of illness. Patients already feel let down by the medical system. When mindset tools don't work in practice, it compounds that defeat. BeHere.health content acknowledges that cost directly and addresses the gap without adding another thing patients have to push through alone.

## Content Pillars

**Pillar 1: Long COVID Practice Reality (40%)**
Targeting Clusters 1, 5, and 7 from SEO strategy. Content that addresses the gap between understanding Long COVID mindset approaches and executing them during actual symptom flares. Primary audience: Long COVID patients who have encountered acceptance-based frameworks but struggle with solo implementation. Covers what to do when traditional mindset advice collides with post-viral symptom unpredictability.

**Pillar 2: Practice Gap Solutions (30%)**
Targeting Cluster 7 from SEO strategy. Content addressing the structural problem of executing mindset frameworks without real-time guidance. Primary audience: patients who have completed programs like Primal Trust but are struggling with solo practice implementation. This pillar carries the highest content-tilt concentration and clearest service-page pathways. It leads with the felt problem — "why can't I make this stuff work" — not with clinical or technique language.

**Pillar 3: Post-Viral Acceptance Methods (20%)**
Targeting Clusters 3 and 5 from SEO strategy. Content addressing the surrender-versus-fighting distinction for post-viral conditions. Primary audience: patients across Long COVID, POTS, and ME/CFS who have exhausted medical options and are considering acceptance approaches but resist the "giving up" framing. Content differentiates acceptance from resignation.

**Pillar 4: POTS Nervous System Work (10%)**
Targeting Clusters 2 and 6 from SEO strategy. Step-by-step somatic and nervous system regulation content specifically calibrated for POTS patients. Primary audience: POTS patients seeking body-based practices that account for orthostatic intolerance and autonomic dysfunction. This pillar contains the highest HowTo schema opportunity and targets practice-seeking patients who already understand the mind-body connection and are looking for execution-level guidance.

## Audience Segments

**Problem-Aware (30% of target audience)**
Recent Long COVID/POTS patients overwhelmed by symptoms and just beginning to encounter mind-body approaches. They arrive in crisis mode: the medical system is failing them, their symptoms are unpredictable, and they are scared. They are not ready to buy anything. They are looking for someone who understands what they are living through. That is the only job content has at this stage: make them feel seen before anything else is offered.

The emotional entry point: exhaustion and disbelief. These patients have done everything right — tracked symptoms, pushed for appointments, researched every possible explanation — and still left most appointments feeling more alone than when they walked in. Content must meet that specific exhaustion before introducing anything else.

Search behaviors: symptom-focused with emerging mental health awareness. Best served by: validation-heavy educational content that acknowledges the physical reality first and introduces the psychological layer second. No service page links in content serving this segment.

Where to find them: r/covidlonghaulers and r/POTS on Reddit, early-stage Long COVID Facebook support groups, and HealthRising forums. These are patients asking "why is this happening to me" rather than "how do I fix it."

Pillars 1 and 3 are most relevant. Content types: educational guides, condition-context explainers.

**Solution-Aware (45% of target audience)**
Patients who know mindset approaches exist but are skeptical or haven't found frameworks that fit their specific experience. They've probably tried generic meditation apps, read about ACT, maybe watched a few Primal Trust videos. Something didn't land. They arrive with low trust and a specific objection: "this stuff doesn't work for people as sick as me."

The emotional entry point: guarded frustration. These patients want to believe something will help but have been disappointed enough times that hope itself feels risky. Content must address that specific skepticism directly and immediately — not by promising results, but by demonstrating that BeHere.health understands why the other approaches failed.

Search behaviors: comparison-seeking, method-evaluation, objection-surfacing. Best served by: differentiation content that addresses the "this is different from generic wellness advice" gap immediately. Soft service page links appropriate at the bottom of content serving this segment.

Where to find them: r/covidlonghaulers and r/POTS threads where patients are debating mind-body approaches, Facebook groups where members share what has and hasn't worked, and Primal Trust or Gupta Program community spaces where patients are expressing frustration with solo implementation.

All pillars relevant. Content types: comparison posts, method guides, FAQ content.

**Practice-Seeking (20% of target audience)**
Patients familiar with programs like Primal Trust or Gupta who need execution guidance, not more framework education. They are BeHere.health's warmest organic audience. They have already crossed the belief bridge on mind-body approaches. They are not looking to be convinced. They are looking for help actually doing the work on the days when doing the work is hardest.

The emotional entry point: quiet desperation. These patients understand the frameworks, believe in the approach, and still can't make it work when they're crashing at 2pm. That gap between understanding and execution is demoralizing in a specific way — it feels like a personal failure rather than a product gap. Content must reframe that immediately: the problem is not the patient. The problem is that solo practice without real-time guidance is genuinely hard.

Search behaviors: technique-specific, implementation-focused, troubleshooting-oriented. Best served by: step-by-step practice guides, troubleshooting content, and advanced method explanations written for bad symptom days. Service page links appropriate within the first half of content serving this segment.

Where to find them: Primal Trust and Gupta Program community spaces, Reddit threads asking "how do I actually apply ACT when I'm crashing," and Facebook groups where patients troubleshoot specific practice problems with each other.

Pillars 2 and 4 are primary. Content types: HowTo guides, technique breakdowns, advanced practice content.

**Product-Aware (5% of target audience)**
Patients specifically evaluating BeHere.health or voice-based coaching options. This segment is too small to justify dedicated content investment at this stage. Build the audience first. Product-Aware patients will emerge naturally from the other three segments as content builds trust and drives word-of-mouth in patient communities. Content serving the other three segments does the conversion work here indirectly.

## Content Types & Format Strategy

**Step-by-Step Practice Guides (35% of content)**
1,500-2,500 words maximum. Longer than that assumes a level of cognitive endurance this audience doesn't reliably have. Numbered sections with HowTo schema markup. Serves practice-seeking segment primarily. Maps to Pillars 2 and 4.

Written for bad symptom days — assumes fatigue and brain fog throughout. Structural requirements: maximum 5 steps per guide (working memory limit for this population), 2-3 sentence paragraphs with white space between each, progress indicators at section breaks ("Section 2 of 4"), and time estimates before the first step so patients can decide whether they have the energy to begin. Each guide includes modification options and a troubleshooting section at the end.

Every practice guide includes an audio version linked at the top: "Can't read today? Listen instead (8 minutes)." On bad symptom days, patients can't process text. The audio version is not supplementary. It is the primary delivery mechanism for this content type on this audience's worst days. This directly reflects the voice-first nature of the product itself.

CTA placement: one soft CTA after step 3 ("Try this with voice coaching support"), one direct CTA at the end. Same language both times. Consistent phrasing reduces cognitive load for patients who are already working hard just to follow the guide.

**Method Comparison Posts (20% of content)**
800-1,200 words. Serves solution-aware segment. Maps primarily to Pillars 1 and 3. Compare no more than 2-3 methods per post — more than that exceeds working memory capacity and loses the reader before they reach a conclusion. Uses simple comparison tables with concise cell content. Targets PAA opportunities and featured snippet eligibility.

Each post ends with a single cognitive bridge: "Feeling foggy? Here's the one thing to remember from this page." One sentence. One takeaway. This anchors the content in the patient's memory on days when retention is low and gives them a reason to return.

CTA placement: one soft link at the bottom for solution-aware patients not yet ready to commit. No mid-content service page links in this format.

**Educational Guides (25% of content)**
2,000-3,500 words broken into 3-4 clearly labeled sections with descriptive headers and section progress indicators. Serves problem-aware patients transitioning to solution-aware. Maps to all pillars with emphasis on validation before solution introduction. Targets informational search intent.

Brain fog accommodation requirements: one idea per paragraph, numbered sections so patients can track where they are, descriptive headers that function as standalone summaries for patients who are scanning rather than reading. The opening 150 words do the heaviest lifting — patients with brain fog decide whether to continue reading within the first two paragraphs. Lead with the felt problem, not the framework.

Audio summary embed at each major section break: 30-60 seconds recapping what the section covered. Patients who lose the thread mid-article can reorient without re-reading.

CTA placement: no service page links for strictly problem-aware content. One soft link at the bottom for content serving patients in transition to solution-aware.

**FAQ and Troubleshooting Content (20% of content)**
500-900 words addressing specific execution problems. Serves practice-seeking segment primarily. Maps to Pillars 2 and 4. Targets PAA boxes and specific long-tail problem searches. High internal linking density to relevant practice guides.

Increased from 10% to 20% because troubleshooting IS the content tilt. The execution gap is the problem BeHere.health solves. FAQ and troubleshooting content is where that tilt is most directly expressed in search-accessible format. Every FAQ piece addresses a specific moment of failure: "I tried voo breathing and it made me more anxious" or "I can't focus long enough to do a body scan." These are the searches that signal a patient is exactly one step away from needing what BeHere.health provides.

CTA placement: direct service page link within the first half of every troubleshooting piece. Patients arriving at troubleshooting content are practice-seeking. They are ready.

## Editorial Cadence

**Minimum viable cadence:** 1 piece per week, published on the same day every week without exception. This audience needs to trust that the resource will be there during their next crash. A missed week is a broken promise. One piece published consistently every week for 18 months builds more authority than two pieces per week that falls apart by month three.

**Tilt-anchored publishing schedule:**
Every week includes at least one piece that directly expresses the content tilt: the execution gap, the practice problem, the "why can't I make this work" question. That is the non-negotiable anchor. Supporting content — educational guides, method comparisons — rotates around it.

- Week 1: Practice Gap Solutions piece (Pillar 2, tilt anchor)
- Week 2: Long COVID Practice Reality piece (Pillar 1)
- Week 3: Practice Gap Solutions piece (Pillar 2, tilt anchor)
- Week 4: Post-Viral Acceptance Methods piece (Pillar 3) or POTS Nervous System Work piece (Pillar 4)
- Repeat cycle, with pillar emphasis in weeks 2 and 4 shifting based on keyword cluster performance

**Launch sequence priority:** Begin with Pillar 2 (Practice Gap Solutions). This is the highest content-tilt concentration and the clearest expression of what makes BeHere.health different. Establish authority on the execution and implementation problem before expanding to condition-specific content in Pillars 1, 3, and 4.

**Batch creation strategy:** Write practice guides individually due to research intensity and the precision required for bad-symptom-day formatting. Batch FAQ and troubleshooting content in 3-4 piece sessions for efficiency. Educational guides require individual attention for condition-specific research and voice-of-customer language mining.

**Performance review cadence:** Review keyword rankings and engagement metrics every 60 days for the first six months, then every 90 days. Adjust pillar emphasis in weeks 2 and 4 based on which clusters are gaining traction. Do not adjust the tilt anchor. That stays fixed regardless of what the data says in the short term. Consistency on the core tilt is the only path to content authority.

**Email list priority:** Every piece of content has one job beyond the content itself: grow the email subscriber list. That is the real audience number. Social shares, page views, and time-on-site are secondary signals. The metric that matters is how many people trusted BeHere.health enough to hand over their email address. Track that number weekly. If it isn't growing, the content isn't connecting.
